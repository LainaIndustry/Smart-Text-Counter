How to Write Compelling Email Subject Lines

Learn proven techniques to create email subject lines that get opened and drive engagement with your audience.

Your email subject line is the first thing recipients see, and it often determines whether your email gets opened or ignored. Writing effective subject lines is both an art and a science.

Keep It Short and Sweet

Most email clients display only 50-60 characters of subject lines on mobile devices. Use our character counter to ensure your subject lines fit within this limit.

Tip: Aim for 6-8 words that convey your main message clearly.

Create Curiosity

Subject lines that spark curiosity tend to get higher open rates. Ask a question or hint at valuable information inside.

Example: "The secret to better writing..." instead of "Writing tips newsletter."

Use Personalization

Including the recipient's name or other personal details can significantly increase open rates.

Tip: Use merge tags carefully to avoid making subject lines too long.

Avoid Spam Triggers

Certain words and phrases can trigger spam filters. Avoid excessive use of words like "free," "guarantee," or all caps.

Test Different Approaches

What works for one audience might not work for another. Test different subject line styles to see what resonates with your readers.

Email Subject Line Checklist

  • Keep under 60 characters
  • Create curiosity or value
  • Use personalization when appropriate
  • Avoid spam trigger words
  • Test and optimize regularly
